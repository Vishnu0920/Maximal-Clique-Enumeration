Algorithm Insights from Maximal Clique Enumeration

1. Algorithm Performance

  • Bron-Kerbosch with Degeneracy dominates in speed:
    - Solved Skitter Network (1.7M nodes, 11M edges) in 150.37s vs Tomita's 527s
    - 1,000x faster than Chiba on Wikipedia Votes (914ms vs 781s)
  • Tomita remains viable for large graphs:
    - Handled Skitter Network in 527s despite its massive scale
  • Chiba scalability limitations exposed:
    - Failed completely on Skitter Network
    - 2-3x slower than others on smaller datasets

2. Algorithm Consistency

  • Perfect match on Wikipedia & Enron:
    - Identical clique counts (459,002 and 226,859)
    - Same max/average clique sizes across all algorithms
  • Skitter validation:
    - Bron-Kerbosch and Tomita agreed on 37M cliques
    - Consistent max clique size of 67 nodes

3. Practical Takeaways

  • For large networks: Bron-Kerbosch with Degeneracy is essential
    (Recommended for social/media graphs >1M nodes)
  • For moderate datasets: Tomita provides reliable fallback
    (Suitable for networks <500k nodes)
  • Avoid Chiba for big data: Fails on massive networks
    (Limited to small-scale academic use)
